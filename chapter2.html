Chapter II

The Collector's Echo

✦ ✦ ✦

The rain in Veleslav tasted like ash. Lily traced the condensation on her bedroom window, the distorted streetlights blurring into ghostly halos. She'd been in the town for barely a week, yet an insidious unease had already settled beneath her skin. A feeling of being… watched.

A sharp rap at the door startled her. Old Man Hemlock, the landlord, claimed he'd left a spare key "just in case." Lily hadn't asked for it. She hadn't even seen Hemlock since signing the lease.

The key was antique, heavy brass tarnished with age. It didn't fit the front door. Or any of the windows. Frustrated, Lily almost tossed it aside, then noticed a small, almost imperceptible panel in the wall behind a faded tapestry. A panel she hadn't noticed before.

The key slid in with a satisfying click.

The panel opened to reveal a small, concealed compartment. Inside, nestled amongst dust and cobwebs, was a collection of photographs. Not the cheerful snapshots of family vacations. These were faded, sepia-toned portraits of people she'd never met. Locals, she presumed, judging by the archaic clothing and the somber expressions.

Each face seemed… haunted.

As she flipped through the pictures, a chilling detail emerged. In several of the photographs, a blurred figure lurked in the background — a shadowy form obscured by darkness, but undeniably present. A figure with a lean build, and… a familiar set of features.

A figure who bore an unsettling resemblance to Zane.

Lily's breath hitched. She frantically turned to the back of each photograph, hoping for a date, a name, anything to provide context. Most were blank. But one, attached to a portrait of a young woman with hollow eyes, bore a single, handwritten word scrawled in elegant calligraphy:

Taken.

A sudden gust of wind rattled the windowpanes. Lily spun around. Across the street, partially obscured by the downpour, she saw him.

Zane.

He wasn't looking at the building. He wasn't doing anything overtly suspicious. He was simply standing there, a dark silhouette against the rain-swept street. But his gaze… she felt it. A cold, penetrating stare that seemed to bore straight through her.

He slowly raised his hand and gave a barely perceptible nod.

Then he vanished into the darkness.
