Chapter III

The Collector's Invitation

✦ ✦ ✦

The rain had stopped, but the city still tasted of ash. And it clung to her — in her throat, in her thoughts, in the silence she couldn't escape.

Lily lay in bed, staring at the ceiling, the distorted images from the photographs burned into the backs of her eyelids. She hadn't slept. Each flicker of streetlight through the curtains replayed them — the hollow faces, the blurred figure, the single word.

Taken.

It wasn't fear that kept her awake. Not entirely. It was something sharper. More persistent.

Curiosity.

A quiet, insistent need to understand. She replayed the encounter with Zane over and over, searching for something she had missed — a shift in tone, a hesitation, anything that might give him away. But there had been nothing. Only that steady, unsettling precision. He had seen it in her. The questions. The pull. And now she couldn't ignore it.

She swung her legs over the side of the bed. Staying here wouldn't help. Avoiding him wouldn't help. If anything, it felt like standing still would only make things worse.

She exhaled slowly. She was choosing this. Not out of fear. Not out of recklessness. But because not knowing felt worse than whatever waited on the other side.

She dressed quickly, pulling on a dark coat before stepping out into the night.

— ⸻ —

Café Arabela was nearly empty. A lone couple murmured quietly in a distant booth, their voices barely carrying. The scent of stale coffee lingered in the air, heavier now without the usual crowd to mask it.

It felt different at night. Quieter. Watchful.

It was the same place where she had first seen him. The same place where something had shifted. She hadn't planned to come here. But somehow, it felt inevitable.

And he was already there.

Seated near the window, a cup of black coffee untouched in front of him. There was no surprise in his expression when she entered. Only recognition. As if this moment had already happened — just not yet.

Lily hesitated. Then moved. Each step toward him felt deliberate. Measured. Like crossing a line she wouldn't be allowed to step back over.

She stopped at his table. Her pulse was steady now. Not calm — but controlled.

"I found them."

Zane didn't react.

"I know."

She pulled a photograph from her coat pocket and placed it on the table between them. The hollow-eyed girl. The word on the back. Taken.

"You were there."

A pause.

"In all of them."

"Was I?" he asked, his voice smooth, almost absent of inflection.

"Don't do that."

A flicker of something passed through his expression. Not quite amusement. Not quite interest.

"You're asking questions you're not ready to hear the answers to."

"Then give me something I am ready for."

For a moment, he simply looked at her. Then he leaned in — not abruptly, not aggressively — but with a quiet, deliberate shift that changed the space between them.

"You weren't supposed to find them yet."

The words settled heavily in the air. Lily's breath caught.

"What does 'Taken' mean?"

A pause. Subtle. Measured.

"It means someone didn't leave when they should have."

Silence followed. Then —

"You didn't sleep."

Lily's fingers tightened slightly against the edge of the table. A beat.

"You went through them more than once."

He wasn't guessing. He was stating facts. Watching her. Reading her. And somehow… already knowing.

Lily held his gaze, refusing to look away.

"You think you found something," he continued, his voice lower now, quieter. "But this didn't start when you opened that panel."

A chill spread through her. Slow. Deliberate. Unavoidable. This wasn't something she had discovered. It was something she had stepped into. Something that had already been waiting.

Zane's gaze didn't waver.

"You shouldn't look at things that are meant to look back."

The words lingered longer than they should have. He leaned back slightly, the tension shifting but not disappearing.

"You can walk away now, Lily."

A pause.

"Forget everything."

His eyes remained locked on hers. Unmoving. Unyielding.

"Or you can stay…"

He leaned forward again, just enough.

"…and I won't stop what happens next."

The silence that followed wasn't empty. It was heavy. Charged. And for the first time since this had begun, Lily understood something with absolute clarity: this wasn't about finding answers. It was about whether she was willing to keep going without them.

And somehow — she hadn't moved.
